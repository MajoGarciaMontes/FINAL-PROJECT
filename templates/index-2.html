BREAST CANCER PREDICTION

Team members: Daniel Murillo, Roberto Barron, Luis Paul Garay and Maria Jose Garcia.

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the cells of the breast. It occurs when the cells in the breast tissue begin to grow and divide uncontrollably, forming a tumor or mass. Over time, these cancerous cells can invade nearby tissues and potentially spread to other parts of the body through the lymphatic system or bloodstream.

There are several types of breast cancer, and they can be categorized based on where they start in the breast, their behavior, and the presence or absence of certain receptors. The most common type is invasive ductal carcinoma, which starts in the milk ducts and can spread to other parts of the breast. Other types include invasive lobular carcinoma, ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS), and inflammatory breast cancer, among others.
